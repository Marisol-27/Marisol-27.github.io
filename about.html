Journey in Jeweley Business:
I never imagine starting a jewelry business. Growing up, I am not a fan because I am uncomfortable with the sensation of metal on my skin. The business started when I wear a piece of jewelry that I was not allowed by the church to remove- my wedding ring. After my wedding, the first thing my colleagues and friends asked is where we bought our wedding ring. They complement the design and thought there were diamonds but it was only a pattern to make it look that way. It was made by my cousin who is our supplier and also a wide supplier of gold and silver jewelry in Ongpin. Little did I know that some of my friends want to invest in jewelry, particularly in gold but don't have enough money to buy from a branded jewelry store at the mall or don't have a legit seller that they can trust. They first asked me to buy necklaces, rings, and bracelets for them and that's when I had an idea to make it a business. While doing this kind of business I became aware of the advantage of investing in gold because the value never goes down.
